Inkline — Pitch One Pager

Hook (1 sentence)

Run the needle, keep your hand steady, and finish three walk-in tattoos before your client’s pain tops out.

Player Fantasy

Be the calm, precise tattoo artist who can pull clean lines under pressure while reading the room and earning trust in a gritty little shop.

You feel the satisfaction of “perfect linework” and the tension of one slip turning the session sideways.

Core Loop (10-15 min slice)

  • Greet the next walk-in and review a simple flash design (quick choice: small / medium / bold).
  • Place the stencil on the body area (align + confirm).
  • Tattoo the design: trace linework and add one pass of shading/fill.
  • Manage the client’s pain by wiping/cooling at the right moments (and by not messing up).
  • Finish, apply aftercare wrap, mark tattoo complete (progress + move to next client).

Differentiators

  • Tattooing as a precision skill game: linework feels tactile and “craft-like” without turning into a sim spreadsheet.
  • A single rising Pain pressure meter creates real tension and forces smart pacing (wipe now vs push the line).
  • Short, character-driven walk-ins: each client has a vibe, a boundary, and a reason they’re here—without long cutscenes.

Target Platform

PC (mouse) first, with controller support (Steam Deck friendly).

Comparables + Why We Win

  • *Papers, Please* (high-stakes work loop) + *PowerWash Simulator* (satisfying “clean completion”) energy, but with a tighter “don’t slip” skill core.
  • We win by making the act of tattooing feel visceral and readable in under 15 minutes: trace, breathe, wipe, finish.

Scope Guardrails

  • No open world
  • No economy/currency
  • No crafting tree
  • 3-5 interactions max
  • 1 pressure system, 1 progress metric

Inkline — Slice Spec (Vertical Slice Contract)

Slice Goal

Complete three walk-in tattoos in a single shift, proving the tattooing interaction loop and the pain-pressure system end-to-end.

Start / End

  • Start: You unlock the shop, step into the booth, and the first client is ready in the chair.
  • End: The third tattoo is finished, aftercare is applied, and the “Shift Complete” prompt triggers at the counter.

Objectives (Player-facing)

  • Complete Tattoo #1 (easy): stencil → linework → quick shade → aftercare.
  • Complete Tattoo #2 (medium): slightly tighter linework, higher pain sensitivity.
  • Complete Tattoo #3 (hard): longest trace path + higher mistake penalty.
  • Finish all three before any client’s pain meter maxes out.

Pressure System (one)

Client Pain Meter

  • Pain rises while the needle is active (linework/shading).
  • Pain spikes when you deviate off-stencil or pause too long mid-stroke.
  • Pain can be reduced slightly by using the wipe/cool interaction (limited relief, not a full reset).
  • If pain maxes: client taps out → tattoo fails → slice fails.

Progress Metric (one)

Tattoos Completed (0/3 → 3/3)

Fail States

  • Client Pain Meter maxes out on any tattoo (client stops the session).
  • Player abandons the chair mid-tattoo (walks away / cancels) → counts as fail for the slice.

In / Out of Scope

In

  • One shop interior with 6 linked areas (front, desk, prep, two booths, aftercare/exit).
  • Three tattoo “jobs,” each using the same 3–5 interaction types:
  • Stencil placement (align/confirm)
  • Linework trace (precision follow)
  • Shading/fill pass (broader follow)
  • Wipe/cool (pain relief)
  • Finish/aftercare (commit completion)
  • Minimal HUD: Pain meter + Tattoos Completed.

Out

  • Economy, vendors, skill trees, crafting systems
  • Open world streaming
  • Multiplayer

# Inkline — Build Tickets (Ordered)

## T01 — Slice Skeleton
**DoD**
- Shop map exists with 6 locations and simple navigation start→end
- Player can move between areas and reach the tattoo chair
- “Client ready” state gates the chair interaction (no chair = no job)

## T02 — Interactions (3–5 max)
**DoD**
- Stencil Placement: align + confirm on a body zone (simple rotate/position)
- Linework Trace: follow a stencil path with error detection
- Shading/Fill: second pass with wider tolerance but longer exposure
- Wipe/Cool: short interaction that reduces pain slightly
- Finish/Aftercare: commits job completion and advances to next client

## T03 — Pressure System (one meter)
**DoD**
- Pain meter rises during needle-active states
- Pain spikes on trace errors and on long pauses mid-stroke
- Wipe/Cool reduces pain by a fixed amount with a brief cooldown
- Clear fail when pain hits max (client stops session, retry prompt)

## T04 — Progress Metric (one meter)
**DoD**
- Tattoos Completed increments on successful aftercare completion
- Gate next client spawn/availability off progress (0/3, 1/3, 2/3, 3/3)
- End condition triggers at 3/3 and activates A_SLICE_END

## T05 — Playtest Support
**DoD**
- Debug buttons: reset current tattoo / reset full shift
- Minimal HUD: Pain meter + Tattoos Completed + current client label
- Quick-run mode that skips walking and jumps to chair for rapid iteration

Inkline — Playtest Script (10–15 min)

Setup

Goal: Validate the core loop feels satisfying and the Pain pressure creates real decisions across 3 tattoos.

Build / Mode

  • Build: Vertical Slice (Inkline / pack: `inkline`)
  • Input: Mouse (recommended), optional controller
  • Audio: ON (needle + pain spike cues are part of feedback)

Start State

  • Player starts at L01_StreetFront_SignAndDoor
  • Tattoos Completed = 0/3
  • Pain meter = 0
  • Client #1 is available immediately at the chair after check-in

Tester Instructions

  • Play “as intended” on Client #1
  • On Client #2, deliberately make 1–2 small mistakes to test pain spikes + recovery
  • On Client #3, try to finish without wipe/cool unless pain forces it

What to Record

  • Time to complete each tattoo
  • Number of trace mistakes per tattoo
  • How often wipe/cool is used
  • Where the player felt confused or unfairly punished

Step-by-step Run

  1. Enter the shop and reach the front desk area (L01 → L02 → L03).
  2. Confirm Client #1 is ready and proceed to the chair (L05).
  3. Stencil Placement: align the stencil on the target zone and confirm.
  4. Linework: trace the stencil path until linework completes.
  5. Shading/Fill: complete the shading pass (broader tolerance, longer needle time).
  6. Use Wipe/Cool once (even if not needed) to confirm it reduces pain and has a cooldown.
  7. Finish/Aftercare: commit completion and confirm Tattoos Completed becomes 1/3.
  8. Trigger Client #2 availability and begin the second tattoo at the chair.
  9. During Client #2 linework, intentionally deviate off the line 1–2 times:
  10. confirm pain spike feedback (audio/visual)
  11. confirm the mistake does not instantly fail you, but meaningfully pressures you
  12. Use wipe/cool strategically to stabilize pain, then finish Client #2.
  13. Confirm Tattoos Completed becomes 2/3 and Client #3 becomes available.
  14. Start Client #3 and attempt to complete with minimal wipe/cool:
  15. if pain reaches ~75%, use wipe/cool and continue
  16. Finish Client #3 and confirm Tattoos Completed becomes 3/3.
  17. Proceed to the aftercare/shift-end area (L06) and confirm the slice ends at A_SLICE_END.

Expected Outcomes

  • The player can complete all 3 tattoos in ~10–15 minutes on a first run (with 1–2 retries acceptable).
  • Pain meter behavior is consistent:
  • rises while needle is active
  • spikes on mistakes and long hesitation mid-stroke
  • reduces by a fixed amount when wipe/cool is used (not a full reset)
  • Failure is clean and readable:
  • when pain maxes, the client stops the session
  • the game clearly communicates why the failure happened
  • Progress is correct:
  • Tattoos Completed increments only after aftercare completion
  • the next client gates correctly off the progress counter
  • slice ends only at 3/3 and triggers the end prompt reliably
  • The core action feels “satisfying under pressure,” not random or overly punishing.

Bug Buckets

  • Blocker: Cannot finish tattoo / cannot progress clients / end never triggers
  • Major: Pain meter wrong, wipe/cool doesn’t work, progress increments incorrectly
  • Minor: UI clarity, text polish, missing feedback, small animation/audio issues

Post-Run Questions (ask the tester)

  1. Did the linework feel fair (forgiveness) and satisfying (feedback)?
  2. Did you understand why pain spiked when it did?
  3. Did wipe/cool feel like a meaningful decision or a mandatory spam button?
  4. Where did you feel annoyed or “cheated” by the system?
  5. Would you play “one more tattoo” after finishing the third?

Inkline — Presentation Brief

Audience

  • Indie investors / small publishers
  • Vertical-slice collaborators (programmer + artist)
  • Streamers who like satisfying skill loops

30-Second Pitch

Inkline is a tattoo-parlor precision game: pull clean lines, manage client pain, and finish three walk-ins in one shift. It’s satisfying when you nail it—and tense when you don’t.

2-Minute Talk Track

You play a tattoo artist on a busy walk-in shift. Each tattoo is the same tight loop: stencil placement, linework, quick shading, aftercare. The pressure system is a single Pain meter—mistakes and hesitation spike it, wiping/cooling buys you a little space, and if it maxes the client taps out and you fail the slice. The slice proves the core feel: the “needle” interaction is tactile, readable, and repeatable. Three clients ramp difficulty so we can test tuning quickly. No sim bloat—just one clean loop with tension.

Core Loop (One Sentence)

Stencil → trace lines → shade → wipe/cool to manage pain → aftercare → repeat for 3 clients.

What We Are NOT Doing

  • No long-run management sim systems
  • No shop decorating progression for the slice
  • No sprawling feature pile beyond the tattoo loop

Playtest Goals

  • Does linework feel fair (forgiveness) and satisfying (feedback)?
  • Is pain pressure readable and does it create real decisions?
  • Can a new player finish in ~12 minutes without a tutorial wall?

Ask / Next Step

Greenlight a 1–2 week prototype sprint to validate “linework feel,” pain tuning, and one polished booth scene with three client jobs.
